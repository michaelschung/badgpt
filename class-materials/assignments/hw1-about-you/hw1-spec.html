Requirements

To receive full credit on this assignment, you must fulfill the following requirements (feel free to go above and beyond if you want!):

Code

  • A title at the top of the page
  • 4 sections, each with 2 or more informative sentences about your life
  • 2 different types of header tags
  • Thoughtful use of the <p> tag
  • Thoughtful use of bold and italicized text
  • 3 images; make sure that no single image takes up an unreasonable amount of space
  • 1 list (ordered or unordered)
  • 2 hyperlinks

Style

Your code will also be graded on style, as usual. Refer to the Style Guidelines for details.

Organization

Make sure that you organize your files according to the following specifications:
  • Your entire assignment should be in a folder titled hw1-FirstnameLastname, (with your name, obviously)
  • All images should be placed inside a sub-folder named img
  • If you wish to include any other resources (see the hyperlink labeled "Michael's capstone project" in the example webpage), place those files in a sub-folder named res
    • This is short for "resources," and it's a common standard among programmers

Thoughtfulness

Another small part of your grade will come from the thoughtfulness of your writing. It doesn't have to be a masterpiece of literature, but it's pretty easy to tell if the whole thing was pulled together in 5 minutes right before class.

Tips

Here are some helpful pointers for this assignment:
  • 500px is a good standard width for images
  • If you don't remember how to do something, review your code from class or ask questions!
